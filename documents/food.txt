KASHMIRI FOOD AND RESTAURANTS:

Traditional Kashmiri Dishes:

Wazwan
Wazwan is a traditional Kashmiri multi-course feast.
Cooked by a master chef called Waza.
Famous dishes in Wazwan include Rogan Josh, Yakhni, Tabak Maaz, Seekh Kabab, Gushtaba.
Gushtaba is the last dish served in Wazwan, made of minced mutton in yogurt gravy.
Where to eat: Mughal Darbar Restaurant Srinagar, Ahdoos Hotel Srinagar.

Rogan Josh
Rogan Josh is the most famous Kashmiri dish.
Slow cooked lamb in aromatic spices and Kashmiri red chili.
Available in almost every restaurant in Kashmir.

Kahwa
Kahwa is traditional Kashmiri green tea.
Made with saffron, cinnamon, cardamom, and almonds.
Served in a special copper pot called Samovar.
Must try every morning in Kashmir.
Available everywhere in Kashmir.

Dum Aloo
Kashmiri style Dum Aloo is different from rest of India.
Baby potatoes cooked in yogurt and spice gravy.
Famous vegetarian dish of Kashmir.

Modur Pulao
Sweet rice cooked with saffron, dry fruits, and sugar.
Traditional Kashmiri dessert rice.

Sheermal
Saffron flavored sweet flatbread.
Available in bakeries in Srinagar old city.

Haak Saag
Traditional Kashmiri greens cooked simply with spices.
Eaten with rice as staple food in Kashmir.

Famous Restaurants in Kashmir:

Mughal Darbar Restaurant
Location: Residency Road, Srinagar
Famous for: Authentic Wazwan, Rogan Josh
Best for: Tourists wanting to try traditional Kashmiri food

Ahdoos Hotel Restaurant
Location: Residency Road, Srinagar
Famous for: Wazwan and Kashmiri cuisine
One of the oldest restaurants in Srinagar

Shamyana Restaurant
Location: Near Dal Lake, Srinagar
Famous for: Kashmiri dishes and Dal Lake view

Street Food in Kashmir:
Seekh Kababs at Lal Chowk evening market.
Kulcha with Nakhudh near Jamia Masjid.
Sheermal from old city bakeries.
Fresh walnut and almond from dry fruit shops.

Best Time to Visit Kashmir for Food:
Saffron harvest season: October to November. 
Visit Pampore to see purple saffron fields and buy fresh saffron directly.
