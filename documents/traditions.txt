KASHMIRI TRADITIONAL CLOTHING:

Phiran
Phiran is the most famous traditional dress of Kashmir.
It is a long loose gown worn by both men and women.
Made of wool in winter and cotton in summer.
Women's Phiran is more colorful and decorated with embroidery.
Men's Phiran is usually plain and darker in color.
Phiran is worn with a Kangri, a traditional fire pot kept under it for warmth in winters.

Kashmiri Saree
Kashmiri sarees are famous for their intricate embroidery.
Made of silk and decorated with Sozni and Tilla embroidery.
Famous for floral patterns and bright colors.

Kashmiri Cap
Traditional Kashmiri cap is called Kani Topi.
Worn by men during festivals and religious occasions.
Made of Pashmina wool with intricate patterns.

Poots
Poots are traditional Kashmiri shoes.
Made of leather with embroidered designs on top.
Worn by both men and women on special occasions.

KASHMIRI TRADITIONS AND CULTURE:

Wanwun
Wanwun is traditional Kashmiri folk singing.
Sung by women during weddings and festivals.
Songs are about love, nature, and daily life.

Rouf
Rouf is a traditional Kashmiri dance performed by women.
Performed during festivals like Eid and weddings.
Women stand in two rows facing each other and dance.

Hafiz Nagma
Hafiz Nagma is a classical music tradition of Kashmir.
Performed at weddings and celebrations.
Uses traditional instruments like Santoor, Rabab, and Tumbaknari.

Kashmiri Wedding Traditions
Kashmiri weddings are elaborate multi day events.
Wanwun singing happens the night before the wedding.
Wazwan feast is served to all guests.
Mehndi ceremony is called Maenzraat.
Bride wears traditional red Kashmiri outfit with gold jewelry.

KASHMIRI TRADITIONAL FESTIVALS:

Eid ul Fitr and Eid ul Adha
Most important festivals in Kashmir.
Celebrated with prayers at Hazratbal Shrine and Jamia Masjid.
Special Kashmiri sweets and Wazwan prepared at home.

Shivratri - Herath
Called Herath by Kashmiri Pandits.
Most important festival for Kashmiri Hindus.
Celebrated with prayers and traditional food offerings.

Baisakhi
Harvest festival celebrated in April.
Marks the beginning of new year in Kashmiri Pandit tradition.

Tulip Festival
Held every year in March and April in Srinagar.
Asia's largest tulip garden in Siraj Bagh blooms with millions of tulips.
Celebrates arrival of spring in Kashmir.

KASHMIRI TRADITIONAL FOOD CULTURE:

Kangri
Kangri is a traditional fire pot used by Kashmiris in winter.
Made of clay and covered with wicker basket.
Carried under the Phiran to keep warm.
A symbol of Kashmiri culture and identity.

Samovar
Samovar is a traditional copper tea pot used in Kashmir.
Used to brew Kahwa and Noon Chai.
Symbol of Kashmiri hospitality.

Noon Chai
Noon Chai is pink colored salty tea.
Made with special tea leaves, salt, milk and baking soda.
Drunk every morning by Kashmiris.
Also called Sheer Chai.

KASHMIRI TRADITIONAL CRAFTS:

Numdah Rugs
Numdah are embroidered felt rugs made in Kashmir.
Made by felting wool and then embroidering with chain stitch.
Used as floor coverings and wall hangings.
Where to buy: Craft shops in Srinagar.

Crewel Embroidery
Crewel is a type of embroidery done on curtains, cushions and upholstery.
Uses wool thread on cotton or linen fabric.
Famous for bold floral patterns.
Where to buy: Craft shops in Srinagar and Anantnag.

Kashmiri Silver Work
Traditional silver jewelry is famous in Kashmir.
Includes earrings, necklaces, bangles with traditional motifs.
Where to buy: Old city silver shops in Srinagar.
